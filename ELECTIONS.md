# Elections 

All material here is under the purview of the in-toto governance as described
in the [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md) document. As such, any conflicts in the
claims of these document are resolved in favor of the governance's.

## Elections Process

Elections for the in-toto steering committee shall be held within a month's
time window, covering two phases: nomination and voting.

### Nominations

Candidates can be nominated in one of three ways:

1. By directly contacting an itsc member.
2. Through a comment under the relevant election issue under this repository.
3. Via an email to the in-toto-community mailing list.

Candidates can be nominated, and self-nominate, as long as they are eligible.
Nominations are available for the first two weeks of the election cycle.

### Nomination Eligiblity

All members of the in-toto community can be nominated. A member of the
community is defined in the governance document. In case of ambiguity, sitting
itsc members can cast a vote to determine whether a candidate is elegible or
not.

### Voting process & elegibility

The itsc will indicate where votes can be cast. No platform shall be enforced
in this document. For particular voting locations and records see the document
below.

In order to avoid abuse, only in-toto community members are elegible to vote,
as described by the governance document. The ITSC reserves the right to discard
votes if a voter is not deemed part of the community yet. 

Given the above, a voter's identity will be made known to the ITSC and the ITSC
only. However, their vote should *not* be known to itsc members, as it is not
required to confirm elegibility.

### Counting and results

The sitting ITSC will verify and count the votes at the end of the month, and
announce results within a week of the month's end.

The week following will carry out transition of duties between sitting members
end elected members.
